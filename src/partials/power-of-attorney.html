Power of Attorney

A durable financial power of attorney names someone you trust to handle your financial and legal matters if you are ever unable to act for yourself.

Without one, your family may have to petition a Michigan court to be appointed conservator, which is public, slow, and costly. A power of attorney keeps those decisions in the hands of the person you choose.

What this covers

  • ✓Pay bills and manage your accounts
  • ✓Handle real estate and business matters
  • ✓Work with financial institutions on your behalf
  • ✓Avoid a court-supervised conservatorship
