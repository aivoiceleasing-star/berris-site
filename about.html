About me

When you call, you get me

I'm Ari Berris, and I've been practicing law for nearly 25 years. I help people and businesses with estate planning, business law, and family law, and I also assist clients facing drunk driving charges and personal injury matters. Different areas, but they share one thing: at some point, life hands you something legal, and you want someone in your corner who actually knows what they're doing, and who will take the time to explain it.

That's the part a lot of firms skip. My philosophy is simple: big firm knowledge, with personal experience. When you call the Berris Law Firm, you get me. Not an intake assistant. Not a brand-new attorney still learning the ropes. You get 25 years of experience, firsthand.

I keep my practice deliberately small. Instead of taking on as many cases as I can, I work with a limited number of clients each month. I do that on purpose. I don't believe a lawyer can work unlimited hours and still do quality work and be there when clients need answers. Keeping my caseload manageable is how I make sure you get the attention you deserve, and how I answer most questions myself, whether that's by email, over the phone, or across the table.

My best clients understand that good legal work has value. They know it's almost always cheaper to do something right the first time than to fix it after it's gone wrong. My job is to make sure you understand not just what we're doing, but why, so you can make a confident decision and move forward.

My story

How I got here

I'll be honest about how I got here. I went to law school because I loved to argue. I figured I'd become a litigator, that the courtroom fight would be the fun part. I also grew up surrounded by lawyers; my great uncle, my uncle, and my cousin all practiced law, and part of me simply didn't know what else I'd do.

Then I actually tried litigation, and I learned something important: it wasn't for me.

What I found instead was transactional work, estate planning and business law, and I loved it. I preferred the calmer pace and the thoughtful clients. I liked being the person who helps you get it right before there's a problem, rather than cleaning up the mess after. I still practice family law, but even that doesn't feel like traditional litigation to me. Divorce is personal. It's less about the courtroom and more about helping good people get through a hard chapter of their lives.

The story that crystallized it

Someone close to me lost their aunt and uncle one week apart. The whole family was certain there was a will, but no one could find it. What followed was a fight between both sides of the family, and a probate estate worth about $450,000 that cost more than $15,000 to settle. All of it avoidable. All of it heartbreaking.

That's the heart of how I practice. You can handle things on the front side, calmly, in control, at a fraction of the cost, or you can leave it for the back side, when it's expensive, emotional, and out of your hands. I'd much rather help you on the front side.

Vision

The lawyer people actually want to call

I built the Berris Law Firm around a straightforward idea: be the lawyer people actually want to call. Too often, people put off legal decisions because they expect to be talked down to, rushed through, or handed a bill they don't understand for work they can't follow. I want the opposite to be true here. My vision is a firm where you're treated like a person, where the language makes sense, and where you always understand both the what and the why behind every decision.

I believe in doing things right the first time, because it protects your family, your business, and your peace of mind, and because it's almost always the less expensive path in the long run. I believe most legal problems are cheaper and easier to prevent than to fix. And I believe you deserve a lawyer who has the time to know your situation, not one juggling more files than any one person can handle well.

That's why I keep my practice personal and my caseload limited. It's not the highest-volume way to run a law firm. It's the right way to serve the people who trust me with what matters most to them.

If that's the kind of representation you're looking for, I'd be glad to help.
